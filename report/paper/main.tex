\begin{abstract}
Rapid MRI requires aggressive undersampling, but zero-filled reconstructions produce ringing and blur. I conduct a tiny reproduction of \citet{cole2020analysisdeepcomplexvaluedconvolutional} by training capacity-matched real and complex U-Nets on the fastMRI single-coil knee dataset with equispaced masks at $R=6$ (ACS=20). The pipeline logs zero-fill baselines, training curves, and qualitative slices, and saves publication-ready tables/figures for PSNR, SSIM, and $\ell_1$ comparisons. Preliminary runs suggest the gap between real and complex models may be narrower (or even reversed) at this reduced scale; final numbers will be inserted once the overnight experiments finish.
\end{abstract}
