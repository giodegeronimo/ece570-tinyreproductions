\begin{abstract}
Rapid MRI requires aggressive undersampling, but zero-filled reconstructions produce ringing and blur. I reproduce the core claim of \citet{cole2020analysisdeepcomplexvaluedconvolutional} on a trimmed fastMRI single-coil knee subset by training capacity-matched real and complex U-Nets with equispaced masks at $R=6$ (ACS=20). The complex model delivers a modest lift over the real baseline (25.49 vs.\ 25.10 dB PSNR; 0.887 vs.\ 0.785 SSIM) while reducing $\ell_1$ error. Zero-fill baselines, training curves, and qualitative slices are logged and saved as publication-ready assets. All scripts, configs, and checkpoints are included to make the comparison reproducible.
\end{abstract}
